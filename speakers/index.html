SPEAKERS

Anuradha Pal

First Professional Female Tabla player in the world

Anuradha Pal has been recognised as the “First Professional Female Tabla Virtuoso in the World” by the Encyclopaedia Britannica & the Limca Book of Records. Called the Rhythm Queen, she has been a recipient of the First Ladies’ National Award 2018 from the President of India, among other accolades. She has performed in prestigious music festivals like the Woodstock Festival, being the first female Indian musician to do so. In addition to being a tabla phenomenon, she is a multi-percussionist and a music composer. She is now the brand ambassador of the “Beti Bachao Beti Padhao” campaign, inspiring other women to empower themselves through her musical compositions. "If the male synonym for the tabla is Zakir Hussain, then the female equivalent has to be Anuradha Pal." - Hindustan Times

Arun Krishnamurthy

Passionate environmental activist and conservationist

Arun Krishnamurthy, a passionate environmental activist and conservationist, quit his job at Google to devote himself to his dream of rejuvenating our natural habitats. He has been dedicated to restoring India’s water bodies and has inspired communities to clean their polluted urban lakes. He founded the Environmentalist Foundation of India (E.F.I), a wildlife conservation and habitat restoration group. He has won many laurels for his tireless work. In 2010, he became the British Council Climate Champion and went on to win Google’s Social Impact Award in 2011. His greatest recognition so far has been receiving the Rolex Award for Enterprise in 2012. As writer-journalist Shobha Warrier has rightly stated, he lives to keep the environment alive.

Dr. Shubha Tole

Neuroscientist, professor and principal investigator at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research in Mumbai.

Shubha Tole is a neuroscientist, a professor and principal investigator at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research in Mumbai. The subject of her research is development and evolution of the mammalian nervous system. She has made significant contributions to understanding how the brain's structure and circuitry are formed in the embryo. Her research has won her many accolades. She was awarded The Infosys Prize 2014 in Life Sciences for having discovered common genetic mechanisms crucial to the development of the hippocampus, cortex and amygdala. Being a TEDx and TEDxGateway speaker, she is passionate about sharing her knowledge to inspire the youth.

Dr.J.N.Singh

Longest serving Chief Secretory of the Government of Gujarat

Dr. JN Singh, a 1983 batch IAS officer, is the Chief Secretary of the government of Gujarat. He has had the longest tenure of three years as Chief Secretary and also got an extension of 6 months in addition to the tenure. He has had an experience of over 35 years of working in several State as well as Central Government departments. He has held notable positions such as the Chairman and Managing Director of Gujarat State Petroleum Corporation, Finance Member at the National Highway Authority of India and Joint Secretary in the Textiles Ministry, to name a few. With relentless work, he has succeeded in bringing developments and reforms, and overseeing the execution of major projects in all the sectors he has been a part of.

Major Gaurav Arya

Army Veteran and Journalist on Republic TV

Major Gaurav Arya is a veteran, who served 7 years in the Indian army. He has served in the Kumaon regiment, one of the most decorated regiments. He currently hosts the show Patriot on Republic TV, where he continues to pay his service to the army by giving viewers a glimpse into the lives of those in the Indian army. Being a strategic affairs expert, a poet and an eloquent public speaker, he is also a frequent guest and panelist on Republic TV’s The Debate with Arnab Goswami. On the panel discussions and debates, he lends his views on service to one’s nation and a multitude of other topics relevant to national interest.

Mukesh Raval

Project Head, Statue of Unity

Mr. Mukesh Raval was the project director of the Statue of Unity project at Larsen & Toubro. At almost twice the height of the Statue of Liberty, the colossal statue of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel stands proudly as the tallest statue in the world. It is a tribute to Indian engineering skills, says Larsen &Toubro. It was completed in the record period of time of 33 months with zero accidents. He has also headed other momentous infrastructural projects for L&T Oman such as the German and Buraimi universities, the Salalah Airport, the Ministry of Manpower headquarters, the 2010 Asian Beach Games site and the royal palace Bait Al Baraka.

Praveen Pandita

Master illusionist and mentalist

Praveen Pandita is one of the most famous illusionists and mentalists of Asia. Known for his hit TV show “Mystical Stranger”, a live street magic show, he has also performed in shows like India’s Got Talent and Trick or Real for Discovery Channel. He is the only magician from India to have won Ripley’s Believe it or Not Talent Hunt and represented India at the Ripley’s Museum in London. Combining clever visual effects with a sense of humour, he has been developing his own brand of magic to entertain people of all kinds and age groups with seemingly impossible feats.

Dr Sonal Mansingh

Cultural Iconic Personality, Padma Vibhushan Awardee, Member of Rajya Sabha

Sonal Mansingh is a cultural iconic personality. She has been nominated by the President of India to become a Member of parliament, Rajya Sabha.She is the Recipient of high civilian awards of India i.e. Padma Vibhushan and Padma Bhushan among many coveted national & international awards & honors. She was nominated by Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi in 2014 as a NAVRATNA for Swachh Bharat Mission (Clean India Mission). ‘SONAL’ – documentary made by Prakash Jha won National Award for non-fiction in 2001. Currently she is also serving as Second term, Trustee of Indira Gandhi Centre for the Arts (IGNCA) and the Member of General Assembly, Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR)

Jayanthi Kumaresh

The Jimi Hendrix of Veena

Dr. Jayanthi Kumaresh, called the Jimi Hendrix of the Veena by New York Music Daily, is one of the most illustrious Veena artistes of India. Born into a family that has been practising Carnatic music for generations, it would be fitting to say that music runs in her blood. She has contributed to Indian classical music by founding the Indian National Orchestra and has recorded for distinguished labels such as Times Music. She has been invited to perform at prestigious international platforms like the World Music Centre in New York, Theatre de la Ville in Paris and the United Nations to spellbind other parts of the world with the national instrument of India, the Saraswati Veena, transcending language and nationality barriers.
